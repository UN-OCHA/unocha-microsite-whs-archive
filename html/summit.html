High Level Leaders' Roundtables

Seven High-Level Leaders’ Roundtables provided high-profile opportunities for Heads of State and Government and high-level representatives from other stakeholders to make commitments and announce bold actions in support of the five core responsibilities of the Agenda for Humanity.

Read All Roundtable Statements
  • Political leadership to prevent and end conflicts

    Deliver concrete policy and operational commitments to prevent and end conflict

  • UPHOLD THE NORMS THAT SAFEGUARD HUMANITY

    Mobilize resources and influence to protect civilians in hostilities, enabling humanitarian action and health care

  • Leaving no one behind

    A commitment to address forced displacement Identify concrete commitments to reduce forced displacement, support host countries and better share responsibilities.

  • Women and girls: catalyzing action to achieve gender equality

    Focus global attention on the exclusion of women and girls in crisis decision-making, and the impact of war, displacement, and disasters on their lives

  • NATURAL DISASTERS AND CLIMATE CHANGE: MANAGING RISKS & CRISES DIFFERENTLY

    Natural disasters and climate change: managing and preparing differently Commit to working together to better manage risks, act early and invest in preparedness

  • From providing assistance to ending need

    Secure concrete commitments aimed at changing people’s lives, from delivering aid to ending need

  • Investing in humanity: humanitarian financing

    Commit to guaranteeing minimum resources necessary to preserve life and dignity of affected people, while maximizing the impact of available resources.

 

Special Sessions

Fifteen Special Sessions provided a platform for Member States and other stakeholders to launch strategic initiatives underlying the five core responsibilities.

Read all Special Session Statements
  • People- centered Humanitarian Action

    Invite commitments to keep affected people informed and to ensure that humanitarian responders listen to them

  • People with Disabilities in Humanitarian Action

    Commit to making humanitarian action more inclusive of persons with disabilities.

  • Islamic Social Finance

    Diversify the humanitarian resource base and funding architecture to include new and emerging donors from faith-based funding

  • Migrants and Humanitarian Action

    Seek commitments from source and host countries to effectively protect and assist vulnerable migrants

  • Education in Emergencies

    Seek commitments and concrete actions to prioritize education in emergencies and protracted crisis

  • Response in Global Health Crises

    The “Global Accord on Healthcare for Crises-affected People” will ensure that minimum essential health services are delivered and adapted to crises contexts

  • Global Urban Crises Alliance

    The Global Alliance for Urban Crises will advocate for adaptation of global crisis response to an increasingly urban world.

  • Global Alliance for Humanitarian Innovation

    The Global Alliance for Humanitarian Innovation (GAHI) will turn ideas into action by promoting innovative tools and practices in humanitarian preparedness and response.

  • Humanitarian Principles

    Identify commitments on how to better respect and apply the universality of humanitarian principles

  • Empowering Youth

    Galvanize action to officially engage young people in humanitarian action

  • Religious Engagement: The Contributions of Faith Communities to our Shared Humanity

    Examine activities and comparative advantages of different religious groups in humanitarian action

  • Connecting Businesses

    The Connecting Business initiative will showcase business making an impact in humanitarian crisis, advocating for business engagement and public-private collaboration

  • REGIONAL ACTION FOR GLOBAL CHALLENGES

    Showcase examples of regional approaches to recent crises and identify challenges and opportunities

  • Protecting Journalists and Promoting Independent Reporting in Crisis Situations

    Shine a light on reporting in crisis to identify good practices, consider standards of reporting and prioritize the safety of reporters

  • Improving Risk and Vulnerability Analysis

    Establish a Global Risk Platform mapping and uniting the existing risk, vulnerability and threat analysis initiatives into one global community of practice

Code Of Conduct

We want all members of World Humanitarian Summit to have an enjoyable, friendly and safe experience. Accordingly, all site users and any contributions they make must comply with this Code of Conduct. By engaging with World Humanitarian Summit you accept and agree to abide by this Code of Conduct.

Within this Code of Conduct, "contribution" means any material posted or uploaded to our websites by a member of the public, including any text, photographs, graphics, video or audio material.

General Rules:

Users must not:

  • Use World Humanitarian Summit to send offensive or harassing material to other users;
  • Publish defamatory and/or knowingly false material about any person, other users or entities;
  • Reveal confidential information in a personal online posting, upload or transmission - including financial information and information relating to customers, business plans, policies, staff, and/or internal discussions relating to any entity;
  • Undertake deliberate activities that undermines the purpose of World Humanitarian Summit;
  • Introduce any form of malicious software into the community.

Your Contributions:

  • Must not plagiarize or violate copyright, database, trademark, trade secret, privacy, personal or proprietary rights of any kind. Therefore, users will generally need the explicit permission from copyright owners of any existing articles, news reports, music, video clips or other copyrighted material before posting them to the website.
  • Must not contain unlawful, objectionable, disruptive, offensive, harassing, threatening, defamatory, obscene, offensive, abusive, hateful, inflammatory, profane, racially offensive, sexually offensive, or religiously offensive content. Please be respectful and civil to other members, even if you disagree with them. Debates should be productive and founded on constructive criticism.
  • Must address development related topics. Contributions must not contain unsuitable or irrelevant website addresses or URLs. Links to pornography, commercial or other inappropriate content will be deleted.
  • Must not promote illegal behavior. Contributions must not contain violent or sexually explicit material or advocate, promote or assist any unlawful act such as (by way of example only) terrorist acts, copyright infringement or computer misuse.
  • Must not misrepresent their origins. Contributions and/or user names may not be used to impersonate any other person, to misrepresent your identity or affiliation with any person, or to give the impression that they emanate from the UN when is not the case.
  • Must not involve any flooding, spamming or advertising. Flooding is when the same comment is posted over and over in the same forum; spamming is the posting of the same comment (or very similar) to more than one forum. All advertisements, chain letters, pyramid schemes, junk mail and any other forms of commercial solicitation are prohibited.
  • Are all public and may appear in public search results. Accordingly, users should not post personal details or information that should not be publically available.

Users who violate these rules and contribution guidelines will be sent a warning message by the World Humanitarian Summit team identifying the issue at stake and notifying them of its removal. Users who repeat offenses after receiving warnings will be banned from the site.

If you reasonably believe that any contribution to the platform made by another user contravenes this Code of Conduct and/or any of the Website Terms, please notify us via support[at]whsummit[dot]org

The World Humanitarian Summit team will periodically revise the Code of Conduct from time to time by amending this page. Please visit this page regularly to ensure you are aware of any changes made as they are legally binding.

This is a public and open platform; the information you post here is public and may appear in search results. Therefore you should not post personal details or information you do not want to be available publicly.
